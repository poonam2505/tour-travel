Welcome to Kerala

God's Own Country – where nature, culture, and serenity unite

About Kerala

Kerala, located in the southwestern part of India, is known for its tranquil backwaters, lush green landscapes, pristine beaches, and vibrant traditions. It's a paradise for nature lovers and cultural explorers alike.

Things to Do in Kerala

Alleppey Backwaters: Enjoy a serene houseboat cruise through palm-lined canals and lush paddy fields.

Munnar: A hill station blanketed with tea gardens, misty hills, and cool breezes – perfect for a peaceful getaway.

Thekkady: Home to Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, ideal for jungle safaris, bamboo rafting, and spice plantation tours.

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Kerala is from September to March when the weather is pleasant, perfect for backwaters and beaches. Monsoon (June to August) is great for Ayurveda treatments and lush greenery.

How to Reach Kerala

  • By Air: Major airports in Kochi, Thiruvananthapuram, and Calicut connect Kerala to major Indian and international cities.
  • By Train: Kerala is well connected with an extensive railway network across major cities and towns.
  • By Road: National highways and state highways connect Kerala to Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, and the rest of India.

How to Explore Kerala

Explore Kerala via rented cabs, local buses, and ferries. Houseboats, auto-rickshaws, and scenic train rides are also great ways to soak in the landscapes and culture.
